# Consumer Health Marketing Content Policy (Sample)
**Owner:** Regulatory Affairs + Legal + Medical Affairs  
**Applies to:** Web, social, CRM, eCommerce, packaging support, influencer briefs  
**Version:** 1.6  
**Last Updated:** 2026-01-25  

## 1. Policy Statement
All consumer-facing claims must be truthful, not misleading, and consistent with approved product information and regional advertising codes.

## 2. Content Risk Levels
**Green (Low Risk)**  
- Pure product facts (ingredients, pack size)  
- Label-aligned benefit wording from approved library  
Approval: Brand + Regulatory sign-off pathway

**Amber (Medium Risk)**  
- Implied benefit, consumer testimonials, before/after content  
Approval: MLR required

**Red (High Risk)**  
- Disease claims, prevention claims, comparative superiority, “clinically proven” without citation  
Approval: MLR + Legal required; usually rejected unless fully substantiated

## 3. Prohibited Content
- Disease treatment/prevention claims unless explicitly allowed by local regs and label
- Fear-based messaging (“You will get sick if you don’t…”)
- Guaranteed outcomes (“works every time”)
- Unsubstantiated endorsements (“#1 recommended by doctors”)

## 4. Claims Substantiation Rules
For any benefit claim:
- Must map to approved indication OR accepted monograph + dose rationale
- Must be consistent across channels (web, social, retail)

## 5. Mandatory Disclaimers and Fair Balance
Include region-required disclaimers and avoid burying them:
- Place disclaimers near the claim, not only in footers

## 6. Social and Influencer Requirements
- Provide approved claim set and prohibited phrases list
- Require clear disclosure (#ad, paid partnership)
- No personal medical advice in comments

## 7. Escalation
If content includes:
- comparative language (better, faster, stronger)
- clinical proof wording
- disease references
Then escalate to MLR before publication.

## 8. Audit Logging
All published assets should retain:
- approval ticket reference
- versioned copy
- claim set used
- evidence references
